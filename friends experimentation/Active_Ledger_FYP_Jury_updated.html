02. The Problem

Why Clinical FL Fails in Practice

1. Non-IID Data

Heterogeneous Hospitals

  • Hospitals have vastly different demographics and ECG sensors.
  • Aggregators cannot distinguish legitimate rare cardiac data from maliciously poisoned gradients.
2. Class Imbalance

Erasing Rare Arrhythmias

  • Atrial Premature Beats (APB) are outnumbered 27-to-1 by normal beats.
  • Standard FL optimises for the majority, ignoring clinically critical anomalies.
3. Governance Gap

Passive Defences

  • Aggregators act as ungoverned mathematical black boxes.
  • Passive blockchains only log corruption after the model is ruined, offering no real-time protection.

04. Research Gap

Two Structural Blind Spots

Blind Spot 1

The Augmentation Stealth Channel

  • Medical FL utilizes Generative Models to synthesize rare classes.
  • The Flaw: Geometric aggregators check vectors after local training.
  • A Byzantine client can request APB data, supply a flipped label, and inject poison upstream before aggregation ever happens.
Blind Spot 2

Passive Blockchains Cannot Govern

  • Existing architectures use blockchains purely as an append-only event log.
  • They provide a post-hoc audit trail, but offer zero real-time active resistance.
  • A true defence requires the ledger to actively gate data requests mid-training.

06. The Defence

Proof-of-Contribution (PoC)

Behavioural Scoring

PoC = EMA(0.7) of Accuracy × Participation

  • Historical Memory: Tracks accuracy across all rounds, not just a single round's geometry.
  • Trust Threshold (τ = 0.4): Synthetic diffusion requests are actively denied if the score drops.
Why it beats Geometry
  • The Sleeper Flaw: Attackers behave honestly early on to bypass Krum/Bulyan, then inject stealth backdoors at Round 15.
  • The PoC Catch: Flipping labels natively destroys the attacker's own local validation accuracy.
  • Their EMA score plummets, triggering an immediate active quarantine.
07. Securing Generative AI

Trust-Gated Latent Diffusion

The Blind LDM Baseline

Ungated Synthetic Poisoning

  • Without active governance, the server blindly approves requests for rare-class data.
  • The attacker weaponizes the 1D-UNet, poisoning the dataset mid-training.
  • Maintains a critically high Backdoor Success Rate (BSR).
Active-Ledger PoC

Starving the Backdoor

  • The Approval Daemon checks the PoC score on the blockchain prior to generation.
  • When the sleeper activates, its PoC crashes below τ = 0.4.
  • The generative pipeline is locked, physically starving the attacker of synthetic poison.
08. Methodology

Experimental Setup & Execution Flow

Federated Constraints
  • Scale: 10 Clients, 40 Global Rounds.
  • Hostility: Fixed 20% Byzantine attackers.
  • Skew: Dirichlet Non-IID partitioning (α = 0.5).
  • Workload: 26,400 local epochs simulated across the massive 6-baseline ablation.
Evaluated Attack Vectors
  • Gaussian Poisoning: Blatant noise injection.
  • Static Label-Flip: Colluding semantic poisoning.
  • Sleeper Backdoor: Time-switching, critical-layer injection at Round 15.

Experiment Execution Flow

At the start of each round, 10 clinical silos train a CNN-LSTM locally for 3 epochs on MIT-BIH data. 20% of clients act as Byzantine adversaries applying specific payloads. Gradients are uploaded, SHA-256 hashed, and logged via Web3. The PoC engine calculates historical EMA scores, rejecting poisoned updates and gating synthetic 1D-UNet requests to maintain a target clinical deployability threshold of APB-F1 ≥ 0.75.

10. Latent Diffusion Results

Closing the Stealth Channel (BSR Metrics)

Blind LDM (Baseline)

  • Multi-Krum aggregation with no generator gate.
  • Final APB-F1: 0.857
  • Attacker continuously receives poisoned synthetic data. BSR remains elevated and unblocked.

Trust-Gated LDM (Ours)

  • PoC active governance locks the 1D-UNet.
  • Final APB-F1: 0.879
  • Attacker requests denied permanently after round 15. BSR is structurally starved and blocked.

Security Impact: The trust gate actively interrupts the augmentation stealth channel mid-training, reducing Backdoor Success Rate structurally rather than relying on post-hoc geometric filtering.

11. Peer Review Process

ACISP Critique & ESORICS Response

Every reviewer concern was systematically addressed in the revised submission.

Critique 1: Weak Baselines

Only compared against FedAvg. No evaluation against Krum, Median, Trimmed-Mean, or Bulyan.

Response

Six strong baselines added and evaluated across all three attacks. Full heatmap included.

Critique 2: Informal Adversary Model

Byzantine threat described operationally only. No specification of static vs. time-switching attackers.

Response

Three formally specified attacks: Gaussian (blatant), Label-flip (colluding), and Sleeper (time-switching).

Critique 5: Blockchain Necessity

Since PoC is off-chain, the ledger looks like an append-only log. Why does it add value?

Response

Clarified active governance: It closes the augmentation stealth channel via verifiable on-chain approval receipts.

15. Conclusions & Future Work

What Active-Ledger Proves & Where it Goes Next

Finding 1: Behavioural defence beats geometry

  • Every per-round geometric filter falls below 0.75 APB-F1 in at least one of the three attack scenarios.
  • A sleeper cannot hide from behavioural history because flipping labels degrades the attacker's own validation accuracy.
  • PoC's worst-case APB-F1 of 0.786 is the only result above the clinical deployability threshold.

Finding 2: The augmentation channel is a real attack surface

  • Un-gated diffusion allows Byzantine clients to inject samples from the wrong class manifold, upstream of the aggregator.
  • Trust-gated approval raises APB-F1 from 0.857 to 0.879 and measurably lowers the backdoor success rate.
  • Every augmentation decision is individually auditable from the on-chain receipts after the fact.

Finding 3: Blockchain earns its place

  • The ledger provides tamper-evident behavioural history that a signed append-only log cannot replicate without a trust anchor.
  • Event emission design cuts gas cost from 1,301,000 to 200,375 units, an 84.6% reduction.
  • This makes continuous on-chain logging economically viable for hospital networks operating on real budgets.

Future Work

  • Multi-seed variance analysis to confirm statistical robustness of all reported results.
  • Adaptive adversary model where the attacker is aware of the PoC scoring mechanism.
  • Higher Byzantine ratios beyond the 20% evaluated in this work.
  • Cross-dataset validation on PTB-XL 12-lead ECG and MIMIC-IV.
  • Layer-2 or Hyperledger Fabric for production-scale hospital network deployment.
